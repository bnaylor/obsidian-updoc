# Updoc pivot

~/src/updoc contains the source for `updoc`.  It has some cool features. 

Read the docs there to understand what it is.

It largely has proven the point, and I like the flow, but maintaining an editor
is outside the bounds of what I really want to spend my time on.  There are still
a lot of bugs in that, and it's just not an interesting problem.

We talked about this alternative approach before, and decided against it for
a few reason, but I want to revisit it: make updoc an obsidian plugin, or
a handful of them that work together.

We'll get all the good obsidian stuff for free including a working, maintained-
by-someone-else editor, and bring over the parts of updoc that I like.

## The updoc P0s
- google calendar integration, one-click to make a new note for a scheduled meeting
- google docs syncing
  - smart-ish conflict resolution / merging
- templates & theming
- low-friction experience
- temporal meeting organization + curated note hierarchy
- automatic meeting note naming

## The updoc P1s
- addressbook emulation
- @date/@today support
